Scrambled Eggs
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon milk or water (optional, for creaminess or a lighter texture)
  • 1 tablespoon butter or oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Beat the eggs: In a medium bowl, whisk the eggs, milk or water (if using), salt, and pepper until just combined. Don't over-whisk.
  2. Heat the pan: Melt the butter or heat the oil in a non-stick skillet over medium heat.
  3. Cook the eggs: Pour the egg mixture into the pan. Let it set for about 30 seconds before gently pushing the eggs from the edges toward the center with a spatula.
  4. Form the curds: Continue to pull and fold the eggs until they are mostly set but still slightly moist and creamy. Avoid stirring constantly.
  5. Finish and serve: Remove the pan from the heat and transfer the eggs to a plate. Garnish with fresh herbs if desired. The residual heat will finish cooking them.
